Menus and side Dishes

Date 07:07:2025

What to bring

    Rice Dishes

  • Jollof Rice – A classic, richly seasoned rice dish with tomato-based sauce.
  • Fried Rice – Colorful and flavorful, cooked with vegetables and proteins.
  • Ofada Rice with Ayamase Sauce – Local brown rice served with spicy green pepper sauce.
  • Coconut Rice – Rice infused with coconut milk and often served with chicken or seafood.

    • Soups and Sauces

    1. Egusi Soup – Made with melon seeds, leafy vegetables, and assorted meats, served with swallow.
    2. Banga Soup – Palm fruit-based soup, popular in the Niger Delta region.
    3. Efo Riro – Spicy spinach stew with assorted meats or fish.
    4. Soup – Slimy soup often combined with vegetables and served with fufu.
    5. Oha Soup – Traditional Igbo soup made with Oha leaves and thickened with cocoyam.
    6. Vegetable Soup (Edikang Ikong) – Rich and nutritious, made with waterleaf, pumpkin leaves, and assorted meats.

    Swallow Options

    • Pounded Yam – A staple paired with soups like Egusi or Efo Riro.
    • Amala with Gbegiri and Ewedu – A favorite Yoruba combination.
    • Fufu (Cassava) – Smooth and elastic, served with various soups.

      • Meat and Fish Dishes

      1. Peppered Chicken – Spicy fried chicken, a party favorite.
      2. Asun – Spicy roasted goat meat with peppers.
      3. Grilled Fish – Often seasoned with herbs and spices, served with sides like plantains.
      4. Isi-Ewu – Goat head delicacy prepared with spicy sauce.
      5. Nkwobi – Spiced cow foot dish, a popular Igbo delicacy.

    Side Dishes

    • Moi Moi – Steamed bean pudding with spices and fillings like eggs or fish.
    • Fried Plantains (Dodo) – Sweet, golden-brown plantains served as a complement to various dishes.
